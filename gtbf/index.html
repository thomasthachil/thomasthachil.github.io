What is the Georgia Tech Business Forum?

Running on its 4th year, the Georgia Tech Business Forum works to prepare students for the professional world by connecting them with elite business leaders. Through workshops and speaker series, GTBF provides the opportunities for students to learn tangible business skills to springboard their careers.

This year’s theme is Finding Your Place, encouraging attendees to consider cross-cutting careers and apply their skill set in different industries. Being college students, we often find ourselves searching for our true calling. Sometimes it’s easy to go with the flow and follow a conventional career path. With a community of driven and professionally-minded students from diverse backgrounds, we hope to encourage students to challenge this convention, explore different industries, and find their place.

Learn. Explore. Innovate.


  • Learn from experienced industry professionals.
  • Network with top Fortune 100 companies and professionals.
  • Connect with aspiring leaders of the Georgia Tech community and Greater Atlanta area.

Workshops Led By

Rachel Ford

Director of Techstars Atlanta, in partnership with Cox Enterprises

Rachel Ford began her journey into Atlanta’s entrepreneurial ecosystem while an undergrad at Georgia Tech, co-founding an connected car startup, FIXD, in parallel with her biomedical engineering studies. She was a founding member of Georgia Tech’s Innovation and Design Collaborative and an instructor within Georgia Tech’s Venture Lab, teaching both evidence-based entrepreneurship and design thinking. Named one of Atlanta’s “30 Under 30” in 2015, Rachel has since shifted her focus to establishing Techstars’ presence as the first world-class startup accelerator in the southeastern United States, held in partnership with Cox Enterprises. Techstars Atlanta invests in 10 startups per year, running them through a 13 week accelerator program. Currently, Rachel is a Director of TechStars Atlanta and on the Board of Directors at IgniteHQ.

Jessica Cork

Vice President, Public Relations and Communications at YKK Corporation of America

Jessica Cork is Vice President of Public Relations and Communications at YKK Corporation of America, the parent company of YKK Corporation’s North and Central America Group. Since YKK’s founding in Tokyo in 1934, it has continuously set industry standards for quality, service, value and innovation in the production of fastening products such as zippers, hook & loop fasteners, plastic buckles and notions, webbing, and snaps & buttons, and architectural products such as storefronts, curtain walls, entrances, sun controls, windows and doors. In addition to managing public relations and communications for the Group, Cork serves as the PR and communications liaison between YKK corporate headquarters in Japan and YKK Corporation of America, manages the YKK North and Central America Group’s corporate donations and community outreach programs, provides cross cultural training for Japanese and American employees, and leads YKK North and Central America Group’s Core Values initiative. Cork is fluent in Japanese and has over 27 years of experience studying and working with Japan.

John Patrick

Senior Manager - Academic Strategy

John Patrick has over a decade of experience of career coaching and recruiting in both corporate and career settings. For the past year at Delta, John has shaped the development of university recruitment strategies as a Senior Manager in Academic Strategy. Previous experience also includes working at Scheller College of Business, Turner, VMware, and much more! His professional mission is to leverage his background to support working professionals and students in the pursuit of their dream careers and help them “find their place.”
